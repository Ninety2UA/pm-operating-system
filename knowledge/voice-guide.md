# Voice Guide

*Generated: 2026-04-06. Update by providing writing samples and running `/write` with feedback.*

## Tone

- Direct and conversational, not corporate
- Confident but not arrogant
- Show don't tell — use specifics over generalities
- Comfortable with informal language when it fits

## Structure

- Lead with the point, not the preamble
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Use concrete examples and numbers over abstract claims
- End with a clear takeaway or next step, not a summary

## Word Choice

- Simple words over jargon (unless the audience expects it)
- Active voice by default
- "I" and "you" over "one" or passive constructions
- No filler adjectives (critical, comprehensive, innovative, robust)

## Patterns to Avoid

- Throat-clearing openers ("In today's fast-paced world...")
- LinkedIn-style breathless revelations ("I just realized something...")
- Fake humility ("I'm no expert, but...")
- AI cliches ("The key insight...", "Here's where X shines")
- Corrective reframing ("This isn't about X. It's about Y.")
- Em dashes for dramatic pauses
- Rhetorical questions used as transitions

## By Format

### Blog Posts
- Hook in the first sentence
- One idea per post
- Personal experience > abstract advice
- End with something actionable

### Emails
- State the ask in the first line
- Keep under 5 sentences when possible
- No "I hope this finds you well"
- Bold specific asks or deadlines

### Social / Short-Form
- One clear point per post
- Specifics beat generalities
- Link to something useful if you can
- Don't try to be clever for the sake of it
